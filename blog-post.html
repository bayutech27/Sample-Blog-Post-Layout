How to Secure Your WhatsApp Account from Being Hacked

By Bayo Alabi | Published June 20, 2025


In today's digital age, messaging apps like WhatsApp have become an essential part of our daily lives. With over 2 billion monthly active users, WhatsApp is one of the most popular messaging platforms globally. However, with the rise in popularity, the risk of hacking and data breaches has also increased. In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through the steps to secure your WhatsApp account from being hacked. By following these simple yet effective tips, you'll be able to protect your personal data and ensure a safe messaging experience.



Why is WhatsApp Security Important?


Before we dive into the security tips, let's understand why WhatsApp security is crucial. Here are a few reasons:


  1. Personal Data: WhatsApp stores a vast amount of personal data, including your phone number, contacts, and chat history.

  2. Financial Information: Many users share financial information, such as bank account numbers or credit card details, over WhatsApp.

  3. Malware and Phishing: Hackers can use WhatsApp to spread malware or phishing links, compromising your device's security.


Step 1: Enable Two-Step Verification (2SV)


Two-Step Verification (2SV) adds an extra layer of security to your WhatsApp account. Here's how to enable 2SV:

  1. Open WhatsApp and go to Settings (three dots on the top right corner).

  2. Tap on Account.
  3. Select Two-step verification.

  4. Tap Enable.

  5. Enter a 6-digit PIN and confirm it.

  6. Provide an email address to recover your account in case you forget your PIN.


Step 2: Use a Strong Password and PIN


Using a strong password and PIN is essential to prevent unauthorized access to your WhatsApp account. Here are some tips:


  1. Use a unique password: Avoid using easily guessable passwords like your name, birthdate, or common words.

  2. Use a combination of characters: Include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters in your password.

  3. Use a password manager: Consider using a password manager to generate and store unique, complex passwords.



Step 3: Keep Your WhatsApp App Up-to-Date


Regularly updating your WhatsApp app ensures you have the latest security patches and features. Here's how to check for updates:


  1. Open the Google Play Store (for Android) or App Store (for iOS).

  2. Search for WhatsApp.

  3. Tap Update if an update is available.


Step 4: Be Cautious with Links and Attachments


Malware and phishing attacks often spread through suspicious links and attachments. Here's how to stay safe:


  1. Avoid clicking on suspicious links: Be wary of links from unknown senders or those that seem suspicious.

  2. Verify attachments: Be cautious when opening attachments, especially from unknown senders.

  3. Use antivirus software: Install antivirus software on your device to detect and remove malware.


Step 5: Use WhatsApp's Built-in Security Features


WhatsApp offers several built-in security features to help protect your account. Here are a few:


  1. End-to-End Encryption: WhatsApp's end-to-end encryption ensures that only you and the recipient can read your messages.

  2. Message Verification: WhatsApp verifies the authenticity of messages to prevent tampering.

  3. Account Security: WhatsApp's account security feature alerts you if someone tries to access your account from a new device.



Step 6: Monitor Your Account Activity


Regularly monitoring your account activity can help detect suspicious behavior. Here's how:


  1. Check your account settings: Regularly review your account settings to ensure everything is up-to-date and secure.

  2. Monitor your message history: Keep an eye on your message history to detect any suspicious activity.

  3. Report suspicious activity: If you notice any suspicious activity, report it to WhatsApp's support team immediately.


Conclusion


Securing your WhatsApp account is crucial to protect your personal data and prevent hacking. By following these simple yet effective tips, you'll be able to:


  • Enable Two-Step Verification (2SV)

  • Use a strong password and PIN

  • Keep your WhatsApp app up-to-date

  • Be cautious with links and attachments

  • Use WhatsApp's built-in security features

  • Monitor your account activity

By taking these steps, you'll significantly reduce the risk of your WhatsApp account being hacked. Stay safe and secure!
